An existing product

My time at Developerhub was the first time I joined and existing product, working in a number of different projects: Improving the navigation on mobile, redesign of the sign up/ log in page (check it out here), history view feature, the creation of illustrations for its 404 and 403 page and a number of promotional images.

Our user base were technology companies (we call them writers) that needed to make their documentation available to their user base (readers)

Autogenerated landing page

The creation of an autogenerated landing page was one of the projects in which I worked from ideation to working software

The objetive was to provide our writing users with a modern and smart entry point where readers could understand the contents of their Developer Hub Documentation right from the start.

The challenge was for this feature to not create any extra work for the writers, having the landing page to adapt automatically to the content and auto-generate according to the structure of the documentation.

Solve one, extend to many

The problem was: with great customisation comes great difficulty to create any standards!

Our clients documentations where organised on references, docs, versions, categories, pages and subpages. Some used categories as pages and pages as subpages, others used versions as different docs… everyone had their onw method.

On the colours side the situation was similar, some users used the same (or really similar) colour for both main and links, others used white as main colours (with looks transparent) some used black for both their colours.

Sketch

After lots of whiteboarding structures and wireframes and once that I had a clearer idea of the way things were going to be organised I started designing iterations for the display of the page.

I choose one of our customers with a brighter colour as a example as I designed the possible look of the page, and possible adding of images, icons and call to actions.

The main objective on this project was allowing clients to have a landing page without having to Do any extra work at the same time the branding of the page had to be recognisable.

Share!

Before building we shared the design with some of our users

From our most active users we aim to select the range with the most varied way of organising their documentation to maker sure all needs were covered

Once we knew we were in the right track respecting the design of the page we starting designing the details for personalising the page, the interactions and how this new feature will fit inside the dashboard.

Allowing further customisation

The Landing Page submenu allows for further customisation of the Landing Page by allowing users to choose an overlay for their hero image

Writers also have to option of disabling the landing page if they wish

The Hero text can be changed directly on the page, as the option of creating a call to action button and choosing where the button links
