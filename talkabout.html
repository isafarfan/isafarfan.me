The task

Talkabout is a community of individuals, groups and organisations who share knowledge through reciprocal talks.

The project (at the moment) consist in creating a splash page that informs users on how to use the page and allow them to register interest to submit or request talks

The first draft for the talkabout project started with conversations with the client and his vision for the platform, some sketches for possible logos were made in a napkin

The logo was to reflect the reciprocability and talk nature of the platform, the client wanted a classic and not overcomplicated look

The Font

Courier is a monospaced slab serif typeface designed to resemble the output from a strike-on typewriter. The typeface was designed by Howard "Bud" Kettler in 1955, and it was later redrawn by Adrian Frutiger for the IBM Selectric Composer series of electric typewriters

Although the design of the original Courier typeface was commissioned by IBM, the company deliberately chose not to secure legal exclusivity to the typeface and it soon became a standard font used throughout the typewriter industry. Because IBM deliberately chose not to seek any copyright, trademark, or design patent protection, the Courier typeface cannot be trademarked or copyrighted and is completely royalty free.

Its because it conections with technology and the spirit of free sharing that surround it that this font was choosen

The page

People visiting the page must be able to find:

  1. What is it. Talkabout is a new platform and users need to know what it is about
  2. How does it works. Depending if they are submitting or requesting a talk
  3. What are the rules.
  4. Keep on the loop. Register their interest and be notified once the page is up and running
