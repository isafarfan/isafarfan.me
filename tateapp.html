Creating tours in the Tate App

Role
  • Research
  • Ideation
  • Prototyping
Tools
  • Guerrilla research
  • Survey
  • Sketch
  • Photoshop
Deliverables
  • Issues report
  • Wireframes
  • Low fidelity prototypes
Timeline
  • 4 weeks

The task

To observe visitors of the Tate Modern gallery in London and identify issues in their interactions with the gallery

To create a design plan that eventualy creates a solution to said issues

The research phase of this project consisted in shadowing different kinds of personas through the gallery and note down if there were any issues

Did they had any issues? Did the found what the look for? Did they seemed frustrated at any point?

The realization of a quick survey to leaving visitors to know more about their experience after the visit

The problem

Problems in the gallery
  • The size of the gallery makes difficult to visit all in one day
  • Most people follows the same route which creates bottlenecks in some areas
Problems in the app
  • There is a very low use of the app by visitors
  • App presents all content in alphabetical order making it really difficult to navegate at the same time that visiting the gallery

Guerrilla research takeaways

Although some issues were observed in the interaction between the users and the gallery, all visitors said to be satisfied after their visit and didn't have any feedback respecting how would they improve the gallery

Most visitors are happy with just wandering around the gallery, this means that the solution for those who don't want to feel lost shouldn't be invasive and be available only for those who require it

During my visit I could only observe one person using the app, and this person was checking a physical map

This gave me the idea of improving the app by reorganising the content to create a seamless experience between the app and the gallery

Also for those visitors that have not much time for their visit, the option to create a tailored route that adapts to their available time or their interest

Given time constrictions the solution to be developed would be the personalised tour through the gallery

Designing a solution

  1. Desk research Learn how many visitors a day the gallery has. Learn the average time spent in the gallery
  2. Personas Create personas for visitors
  3. Benchmarking Research the routes created by other large galleries/museums in their applications and their approach to queue management and organization
  1. Inventory of contents Catalogue the gallery's works with appropriate metadata to power the app.
  2. Card sorting Give visitors cards listing the different works exhibited at the gallery and ask them to group them in order to define the most important works
  3. Affinity diagram Use card sorting and affinity diagrams to create different routes that are relevant and interesting for each persona

The prototypes

Design thinking will be used to:
  1. EmpathizeWhich works or routes are most interesting to each profile
  2. DefineThe routes to follow
  3. DeviseInteraction methods between the application and the gallery
  1. PrototypeThe different routes and points of interaction
  2. TestChecking that the different routes are feasible in the time allocated to them, create heuristic evaluations and cognitive paths for the prototypes to be able to introduce changes before performing user tests.
