The challenge

This hackathon was created by DePaul in collaboration with BCG Digital Ventures to explore new ideas on how to help the organisation and those who benefit from it services.

Depaul is a charity supporting young people experiencing, or at risk of, homelessness; they face multiple organisational challenges so a hackathon was created to create a digital product that helps Depaul deliver services to those in need and generate a bigger impact in the community, both short-term and longer into the future.

Research

Giving the short period of time of which the hackathon was taking place, DePaul carried out an extensive research consisting of:

  1. Interview preparation and planning
  2. Interviews with Depaul staff
  3. Interview with young people who are receiving support from Depaul
  4. Desktop research
  5. Insights synthesis

Every participant received a pdf document containing insights and areas of opportunity:

Insights

Our solution is focused on solving the issues that arise on the areas of Life Projects and reporting and Feedback Collection

  • Young people have trouble visualising and committing to their goals.
  • The support planning process is mainly paper-based.
  • Young people often forget meetings and appointments (e.g. job interviews, doctors appointments) which affects their chances of progressing in life.
  • Despite a reliance on their mobile devices young people struggle with basic administrative skills such as paying a direct debit online
  • Gathering feedback from Depaul users is paper based and not written in a style that is easy for the young people to understand.
  • When young people move on, it is difficult to track their trajectory and keep communication open.

Insights and design of a solution

One of the most frustrating thing for most users of existing apps was to not be able to filter according to their preferences

The lack of time and the cleanliness of their kitchen has an important role in whether they will cook or not

At some point over the conversation and without being prompted users would talk about having food related goals (eating fresher, having more protein, reducing animal product intake)

The app

The users can earn points by interacting with the app

The application works as a goal tracker and a stock control for the user's kitchen, using the user's available ingredients to suggest simple recipes tailored to their goals

The application also provides information about the best way of storing or preserving the ingredients and gives priority to consuming nearly expiring items as a way of earning points

Wireframes, testing and iterations

The wireframes were completed with information and went through and heuristic evaluation, a presential user test and a remote user test

The initial prototypes were really simple and a number of design elements were introduced as the tests took place
