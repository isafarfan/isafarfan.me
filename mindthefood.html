The problem

Food waste is both a global and local issue. The annual global food waste weights 1.3 billion tonnes, this food let to rod, emits green house gases.

In the UK, well over the half of the food waste comes from households. The main reason why food goes to waste is because it hasn't been consumed on time.

This waste could be avoided by a better planning of shopping and cooking meals, but this is difficult to achieve in a fast paced city as London. The avoidable food waste almost double for people living alone, compared to people sharing their meals.

The Task

The task at hand was to create a platform to avoid food waste at home that can be attractive to young professionals

I started my research by doing a field study with organisations that fight food waste in different ways, where I meet people that simpatise with the cause and learned more about their habits

Research Phase

The research phase consisted of field study, bench marking, user selection and open interviews

The criteria to select users for the interviews was a mix of people who like to cook, people who uses any of the existing platforms, people who are conscious about food waste

The reason behind this selection was to find out what users like/dislike from existing platforms and would make people who doesn't use any platform start using one

At the end of their interview the users would play a game to discover how much they know about cooking, storaging and preserving different food items and what would they do in each case

Insights and design of a solution

One of the most frustrating thing for most users of existing apps was to not be able to filter acording to their preferences

The lack of time and the cleanliness of their kitchen has an important role in wheter they will cook or not

At some point over the conversation and without being prompted users would talk about having food related goals (eating fresher, having more protein, reducing animal product intake)

The app

The users can earn points by interacting with the app

The application works as a goal tracker and a stock control for the user's kitchen, using the user's available ingredients to suggest simple recipes tailored to their goals

The application also provides information about the best way of storaging or preserving the ingredients and gives priority to consuming nearly expiring items as a way of earning points
